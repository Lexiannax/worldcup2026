Getting In: every way to reach the stadium

Train, BART, light rail, rideshare, parking and the gates. What to do, step by step, before you leave home.

The one rule

The stadium sits next to the Great America / Levi's Stadium VTA light rail station, about a 3-minute walk from the gates. Every train route below ends with VTA light rail. Driving is the slowest option on match days. If you remember one thing: finish your trip on VTA.

Step by step, from wherever you start

BART From San Francisco, Oakland, SFO or the East Bay

  1. Board any BART train toward Berryessa (Green or Orange line; transfer if needed).
  2. Get off at Milpitas Station.
  3. Cross the pedestrian bridge to VTA Light Rail Orange Line.
  4. Ride to Lick Mill Station, then follow the crowd and signage to the stadium.
  5. Going home: follow the Gate F signs to VTA. BART and VTA run matched 20-minute frequencies for timed transfers. Late matches: BART runs special after-midnight service; last all-station train leaves Milpitas at 11:53pm, last train to Berryessa at 1:47am.
Load a Clipper card before you go. One card covers BART, VTA and Caltrain.

Caltrain From San Francisco or the Peninsula

  1. Board Caltrain southbound and get off at Mountain View Station.
  2. Transfer to the VTA Orange Line for the roughly 30-minute light rail leg to the stadium.
  3. Going home: follow the Gate A signs for the Caltrain connection.
A combined Caltrain + VTA day pass covers both legs, roughly $15–20 round trip.

ACE From the Central Valley and Tri-Valley

  1. ACE runs direct service toward the stadium from Stockton, Lathrop/Manteca, Tracy, Vasco, Livermore, Pleasanton and Fremont, connecting to VTA for the final leg.
  2. Lowest-stress option if you live along the Altamont corridor. Check match-day schedules in advance.

Capitol Corridor From Sacramento and the North Bay corridor

  1. Ride Capitol Corridor to Santa Clara / Great America and follow event signage to the stadium.
  2. Check the Capitol Corridor World Cup schedule; match-day service varies by kickoff time.

VTA From San Jose, Santa Clara, Sunnyvale, Milpitas, Campbell

  1. Orange and Green lines both stop at Great America / Levi's Stadium Station, 0.2 miles from the gates. Fare $2.50 each way.
  2. Walk off the train and into the plaza in about three minutes.

Driving, parking and rideshare

OptionWhat to know
ParkingAvailable on match day but advance reservation is strongly encouraged through the official FIFA World Cup 2026 parking page. Expect heavy traffic and long exit times. Some off-stadium lots are a 15–20 minute walk. One parking pass per match ticket.
Rideshare pickupBegins one hour after kickoff. Exiting Gates A, E or F: go to Rideshare North, Red Lot 7. Exiting Gates B or C: go to Rideshare South, Freedom Circle. Autonomous vehicles (Waymo etc.) are not allowed in the rideshare lots.
FreewaysI-880, US-101, CA-87 and I-680, with Tasman Drive providing direct access. Plan extra time both directions.

Accessibility

All gates are accessible; your designated entry is printed on your ticket. Accessible pickup and drop-off is on Patrick Henry Drive. Guests with mobility needs using rideshare should use Rideshare North and will be shuttled to the Stadium Plaza drop-off. Elevators are in the NE, NW, SE, SW, E and W sections and run throughout the match. Accessible parking requires a pre-paid pass plus a DMV disability plate or placard at lot entry. Accessible seating and parking questions: +1 415 464 9377 ext. 4. Wheelchair assistance: +1 408 579 4610. For help during a match, visit the nearest Fan Info Point.

Before you leave home

  1. Check your ticket for your designated entry gate.
  2. Load a Clipper card or buy a transit day pass.
  3. Review the bag policy on the official FIFA Bay Area site; small clear bags only.
  4. Arrive early. Gates and security screening move in waves; the hour before kickoff is the crush.
  5. Pick your exit plan now: Gate F for VTA/BART, Gate A for Caltrain, B/C for Rideshare South.
