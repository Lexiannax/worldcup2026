FIFA World Cup 2026

ALL 48 TEAMS

Coach, star player, strengths and weaknesses for every squad at the tournament. Data sourced from The Guardian's complete player guide.

Source: The Guardian — World Cup 2026: guide to all 1,248 players  |  Marcus Christenson, David Hills, Steven Bloor & Garry Blight
Group:
A
Group A
Czechia
FIFA #41
Coach
Miroslav Koubek
Patrik Schick
Strength
Experienced spine led by Soucek; Schick scored 16 Bundesliga goals this season.
Weakness
Lack of technical players; relies too heavily on physicality and set pieces.
Profiles by Jan Palicka / Seznamzpravy.cz
Mexico
FIFA #15
Coach
Javier Aguirre
Raúl Jiménez
Strength
Flexible tactics, tight defensive unit, aggressive pressing and quick transitions.
Weakness
Co-host pressure could affect the squad; Aguirre's pragmatic approach has drawn criticism.
Profiles by Jesús Valdéz / Claro Sports
South Africa
FIFA #60
Coach
Hugo Broos
Teboho Mokoena
Strength
Revitalised under Broos, knitted together by Mokoena and built on well-drilled teamwork.
Weakness
Mainly domestic players lacking wider experience; will rely on counterattacks.
Profiles by Yanga Sibembe / Daily Maverick
South Korea
FIFA #25
Coach
Hong Myung-bo
Son Heung-min
Strength
Robust core, great team spirit and a proper talisman in former PL golden boot winner Son.
Weakness
Uncertainty over ideal formation; late switch to 3-4-3 plus fitness fears over key players.
Profiles by Hyung Seo / Footballist
B
Group B
Bosnia & Herzegovina
FIFA #64
Coach
Sergej Barbarez
Edin Dzeko
Strength
Aggressive defending, direct football, quick transitions and huge young talent in Alajbegovic.
Weakness
Intensity can go too far with loss of shape; unlikely to dominate games in their group.
Profiles by Sasa Ibrulj / Skaut Sport
Canada
FIFA #30
Coach
Jesse Marsch
Alphonso Davies
Strength
Consistent 4-4-2 built on pressing, pace out wide and a resilient defensive unit.
Weakness
Fitness fears over Davies; burden of never having won a World Cup match.
Profiles by Gangué-Ruzic & Kristian Jack / Onesoccer
Qatar
FIFA #55
Coach
Julen Lopetegui
Akram Afif
Strength
Lopetegui brings experience; focused on absorbing pressure and hitting opponents on the break.
Weakness
Conceded 24 times in 10 qualifying games; regional conflict disrupted preparation.
Profiles by John Duerden
Switzerland
FIFA #19
Coach
Murat Yakin
Granit Xhaka
Strength
Topped qualifying group; mix of experience and fresh talent knitted together by Xhaka.
Weakness
Tactical uncertainty over formation; squad lacks depth beyond the starting XI.
Profiles by Christian Finkbeiner / Blick
C
Group C
Brazil
FIFA #6
Coach
Carlo Ancelotti
Vinícius Júnior
Strength
Built around Vinícius with solid spine and attacking flair in aggressive 4-2-4 formation.
Weakness
Full-backs don't match former greats; 34-year-old Neymar may struggle due to injuries.
Profiles by Gustavo Faldon / Estadão
Haiti
FIFA #82
Coach
Sébastien Migné
Wilson Isidor
Strength
Disciplined side with pace and skill to counter; Bellegarde and Isidor bring proven quality.
Weakness
Instability in buildup; striker Nazon held up in Iran and Migné unable to visit the country.
Profiles by Pierre Richard Midy
Morocco
FIFA #7
Coach
Mohamed Ouahbi
Achraf Hakimi
Strength
Setup creates space for Hakimi; reaching the 2022 semi-finals has boosted collective belief.
Weakness
Struggled under pressure at Afcon; will face close scrutiny again this summer.
Profiles by Amine El Amri / Skwad by 2M TV
Scotland
FIFA #43
Coach
Steve Clarke
Scott McTominay
Strength
Built around McTominay and Robertson; organised and experienced first-choice XI.
Weakness
Lacks a reliable scorer; Gilmour injury hurts a squad already short on depth.
Profiles by Ewan Murray
D
Group D
Australia
FIFA #27
Coach
Tony Popovic
Jackson Irvine
Strength
Improved in qualifying, tougher to beat; Irankunda leads a dangerous counterattacking unit.
Weakness
Lack of high-profile names in a transitional squad; 'underdog' tag hard to shake.
Profiles by Lynch, Khan, Pegan & Hytner
Paraguay
FIFA #40
Coach
Gustavo Alfaro
Julio Enciso
Strength
Physical style with tenacity to dig out unlikely results; built on Paraguayan DNA.
Weakness
Only two teams scored fewer goals in Conmebol qualifying; over-reliance on Enciso.
Profiles by Pérez & Gómez / VS Sports
Turkey
FIFA #22
Coach
Vincenzo Montella
Arda Güler
Strength
Harmonious squad with in-form players; Güler and Yildiz are two of Europe's brightest talents.
Weakness
No proven striker; risk of imploding under pressure as at Euro 2020.
Profiles by Emre Sarigul
USA
FIFA #16
Coach
Mauricio Pochettino
Christian Pulisic
Strength
Core squad are 'glue guys' with World Cup experience; Pochettino knows how to win knockout games.
Weakness
Pochettino still unclear on best team and tactical setup; home fans frustrated.
Profiles by Jeff Rueter
E
Group E
Curaçao
FIFA #83
Coach
Dick Advocaat
Leandro Bacuna
Strength
Advocaat's return is a huge bonus; brought in players from the island's diaspora.
Weakness
Possession style may need overhaul; opening against Germany is particularly daunting.
Profiles by Arthur Renard
Ecuador
FIFA #24
Coach
Sebastián Beccacece
Moisés Caicedo
Strength
Defence conceded just 5 goals in 18 qualifiers; Bielsa-esque pressing mentality introduced.
Weakness
Scoring remains a problem; beyond Valencia, attacking options are talented but raw.
Profiles by Francisco Limongi / Studio Fútbol
Germany
FIFA #10
Coach
Julian Nagelsmann
Florian Wirtz
Strength
Rising stars and reliable stalwarts; enough creative talent for a decent group-stage draw.
Weakness
Not yet vintage Germany; inconsistent results and Neuer back in goal without a clear successor.
Profiles by Horn & Fritsch / Die Zeit
Côte d'Ivoire
FIFA #34
Coach
Emerse Faé
Nicolas Pépé
Strength
Settled, flexible defence that didn't concede in qualifying; wealth of attacking talent available.
Weakness
Questions over midfield; underwhelming Afcon defence lowered expectations.
Profiles by Prince Akabla / Le Kpakpato Sportif
F
Group F
Japan
FIFA #18
Coach
Hajime Moriyasu
Takefusa Kubo
Strength
Cohesive pressing unit in Moriyasu's settled system; now have squad depth for a deeper run.
Weakness
Mitoma injury is a blow; tough group with three experienced and physical opponents.
Profiles by Takashi Ogami / Shukyu Magazine
Netherlands
FIFA #8
Coach
Ronald Koeman
Virgil van Dijk
Strength
Smashed 27 goals to qualify unbeaten; back four marshalled by Van Dijk looks impressive on paper.
Weakness
Key players have suffered injury layoffs; facing Japan first will test cohesion.
Profiles by Marcel van der Kraan / De Telegraaf
Sweden
FIFA #38
Coach
Graham Potter
Alexander Isak
Strength
Two elite finishers up front; Potter sets up with hard-working back five to complement firepower.
Weakness
Isak had a grim first season at Liverpool; Potter had limited time after taking over last October.
Profiles by Samuel Parts / Fotbollskanalen
Tunisia
FIFA #46
Coach
Sabri Lamouchi
Hannibal Mejbri
Strength
Did not concede in qualifying; tactically flexible and bringing young talent through.
Weakness
Low-key team lacking elite experience; Mejbri may struggle with freedom in a tough group.
Profiles by Ahmed Adala / Radio Mosaïque FM
G
Group G
Belgium
FIFA #9
Coach
Rudi Garcia
Kevin De Bruyne
Strength
De Bruyne, Doku and Lukaku offer a serious attacking edge.
Weakness
Defence is the weak point; golden generation of Alderweireld, Kompany et al. long gone.
Profiles by Ludo Vandewalle / Het Nieuwsblad
Egypt
FIFA #29
Coach
Hossam Hassan
Mohamed Salah
Strength
Cohesive, hard to score against and emotionally committed.
Weakness
Looks blunt if opponents double up on Salah and midfield cannot pass through the press.
Profiles by Saher Ahmed / Kingfut.com
Iran
FIFA #21
Coach
Amir Ghalenoei
Mehdi Taremi
Strength
Tactically flexible; played 3-6-1 and 4-4-2 in March friendlies against Nigeria and Costa Rica.
Weakness
Based in Mexico but plays all three group games in the US; logistical disruption a real risk.
Profiles by Behnam Jafarzadeh / The Guardian
New Zealand
FIFA #85
Coach
Darren Bazeley
Chris Wood
Strength
Optimism in camp; belief they can reach the knockouts for the first time. Evolved from part-timers to professionals.
Weakness
Wood has had an injury-plagued 2026; recent friendly results mixed at best.
Profiles by Maree Mahony / RNZ
H
Group H
Cape Verde
FIFA #68
Coach
Pedro 'Bubista' Leitão Brito
Dailon Livramento
Strength
Settled squad together nearly five years; physical and defensively solid but blessed with technical forwards.
Weakness
Key defender Logan Costa has been out with ACL injury all season.
Profiles by Alasdair Howorth
Saudi Arabia
FIFA #61
Coach
Georgios Donis
Salem Al-Dawsari
Strength
Low expectations; Donis focused on defensive solidity with pressure largely off.
Weakness
Toothless up front; just seven goals in 10 qualifying games.
Profiles by John Duerden
Spain
FIFA #2
Coach
Luis de la Fuente
Lamine Yamal
Strength
Yamal and Williams inject freshness into a team that still dominates the ball; frontline hard to contain.
Weakness
Back four can have its moments — but this is a fiercely competitive and united team.
Profiles by Nadia Tronchoni / El País
Uruguay
FIFA #17
Coach
Marcelo Bielsa
Federico Valverde
Strength
Bielsa-ball: expect La Celeste to press high and chase the ball at all times.
Weakness
Lack the scoring power of old; hopes pinned on Núñez who has struggled for form at Al-Hilal.
Profiles by Luis Inzaurralde / El Observador
I
Group I
France
FIFA #1
Coach
Didier Deschamps
Kylian Mbappé
Strength
Remarkable front four; back four is strong too. Bench may be the deepest at the tournament.
Weakness
Deschamps' conservatism; the challenge of putting all the puzzle pieces together.
Profiles by Luke Entwistle / GFFN
Iraq
FIFA #57
Coach
Graham Arnold
Aymen Hussein
Strength
Nothing to lose; pressure is on the other three teams in the group.
Weakness
Not free-scoring; breaking down three tough group defences will be a huge challenge.
Profiles by Hassanin Mubarak
Norway
FIFA #31
Coach
Ståle Solbakken
Erling Haaland
Strength
Haaland ready to grab his World Cup moment; averaged nearly five goals a game in qualifying.
Weakness
Tough group demands immediate results; first-choice goalkeeper barely played for his club this season.
Profiles by Stamsø-Møller & Bjelland / TV 2 Norway
Senegal
FIFA #14
Coach
Pape Thiaw
Sadio Mané
Strength
Flexible tactical approach; speed in attack will cause teams plenty of problems.
Weakness
Recent tournament record, aside from the most recent Cup of Nations, is unimpressive.
Profiles by Omar Kane / Taggat
J
Group J
Algeria
FIFA #28
Coach
Vladimir Petkovic
Riyad Mahrez
Strength
Technical ability throughout; Afcon showed how good they can be at their best.
Weakness
Afcon quarter-final collapse against Nigeria raises questions about performance under pressure.
Profiles by Maher Mezahi / DZfoot
Argentina
FIFA #3
Coach
Lionel Scaloni
Lionel Messi
Strength
Almost all of the class of 2022 still around, including Messi.
Weakness
Players had injury setbacks after demanding club seasons; physical condition is a concern.
Profiles by Pestarino, Claus & Zabala / Olé
Austria
FIFA #23
Coach
Ralf Rangnick
Konrad Laimer
Strength
Continuity; Austria's plan has been stable for years and Rangnick has had time to imprint his ideas.
Weakness
Structure slipping leaves them exposed; pre-tournament Baumgartner injury is a huge blow.
Profiles by Andreas Hagenauer / Der Standard
Jordan
FIFA #63
Coach
Jamal Sellami
Mousa Al-Tamari
Strength
Quick transitions and the pace of star winger Al-Taamari; will sit deep in a back five.
Weakness
Striker Yazan Al-Naimat ruled out injured; big-game experience in short supply.
Profiles by Ayman Hijleh / Al-Ghad
K
Group K
Colombia
FIFA #13
Coach
Néstor Lorenzo
Luis Díaz
Strength
Goals not a problem; Suárez prolific in Portugal and only Argentina scored more in Conmebol qualifying.
Weakness
Inconsistency has become a hallmark; tournament football leaves no room for that.
Profiles by José Orlando Ascencio / El Tiempo
DR Congo
FIFA #45
Coach
Sébastien Desabre
Yoane Wissa
Strength
Extremely difficult to break down; mental resilience defined their qualification campaign.
Weakness
Struggle to create in open play; preparations disrupted by Ebola outbreak in the country.
Profiles by Louis Mukoma / Leopardsfoot.com
Portugal
FIFA #5
Coach
Roberto Martínez
Cristiano Ronaldo
Strength
Settled core with varied and versatile options; squad mourning Diogo Jota has a personal mission.
Weakness
Risk of Ronaldo's outsized influence becoming a problem.
Profiles by Nuno Travassos / A Bola
Uzbekistan
FIFA #50
Coach
Fabio Cannavaro
Abdukodir Khusanov
Strength
Solid 5-2-3 defence; conceded just seven goals in 10 qualifying games.
Weakness
Cannavaro's managerial career has been mixed; goals are likely to be a problem.
Profiles by Narzulla Saydullaev / Championat Asia
L
Group L
Croatia
FIFA #11
Coach
Zlatko Dalic
Luka Modrić
Strength
Bags of experience; winning mentality proven at past World Cups. Modric remains a midfield maestro.
Weakness
Can the old guard still cut it? All that experience means plenty of miles in the legs.
Profiles by Alex Holiga / Telesport
England
FIFA #4
Coach
Thomas Tuchel
Harry Kane
Strength
Enviable attacking options, host of No 10s, and in Rice one of the world's best midfielders.
Weakness
Question marks over defensive options; fitness concerns over key players as always.
Profiles by David Hills
Ghana
FIFA #73
Coach
Carlos Queiroz
Antoine Semenyo
Strength
Major threat from set pieces; Semenyo is one of the most in-form attackers in European club football.
Weakness
Lack of elite defenders; goalkeeping unit lacks international experience.
Profiles by Victor Atsu Tamakloe / Myjoyonline.com
Panama
FIFA #33
Coach
Thomas Christiansen
Michael Murillo
Strength
Squad has been together a long time; full of confidence with solid Gold Cup and Nations League showings.
Weakness
Struggle against bigger teams; key players now well into their 30s.
Profiles by José Miguel Domínguez Flores
