Written by someone who actually works match day security here. Real photos. Real tips. No guesswork.

⚽ 9 Matches · June–July 2026
Insider tip: Parking is far from the gates. Budget at least 20 minutes walking from most lots. Take transit if you can — it drops you right at Gate A (west, Caltrain) or Gate F (east, BART).
Quick Reference
Transit

Getting Here

  • VTA to Caltrain → Gate A (west)
  • VTA to BART → Gate F (east)
  • Trains run after every match
  • Tap and ride with contactless card
Parking

If You Drive

  • Lots are 0.5–1+ mile from gates
  • Lot 9 is visible from the scoreboard
  • Arrive 2+ hours early on match day
  • Cash and card lots available
Bags

What to Bring

  • Clear bag 12×6×12 max
  • 1 gallon clear zip-lock OR
  • Small clutch 4.5×6.5 max
  • All bags subject to search
Fan Band

Bank of America Fan Band

  • Pick up outside the stadium
  • Bank of America kiosk in parking area
  • Wristband for cashless payments inside
  • Find it before entering gates
Sun & Shade Guide

Where you sit determines how hot your match day gets. Santa Clara summer sun is intense at noon kick-off times.

☁️

West Side — Sections 130s–146, 230s–246

Shade after midday. Best choice for afternoon and evening matches. Home side.

☀️

East Side — Sections 108–131, 207–231

Full sun, hot all day. Visitor side. Bring sunscreen and a hat.

🌤️

South End — Sections 126–131, 226–231

Sun overhead at midday. Gets some afternoon shade from the upper deck.

🏟️

North End — Sections 101–106, 201–206

Behind the goal. Upper deck provides cover from direct overhead sun.

Parking

If you drive, know what you are getting into. The lots are large. The walk is long.

Distance

How far is parking?

Most parking lots are 0.5 to 1+ miles from the stadium gates. On a hot June afternoon, that walk is significant. Lot 9 (marked with a green sign) is visible from the San Francisco Bay Area Stadium scoreboard.

Arrive Early

Timing

Arrive at least 2 hours before kick-off if driving. The surrounding roads get congested fast. Security checks also add time, especially at peak times.

About this site

This fan guide is written and maintained by Alexandra McKeever, a security officer working FIFA World Cup 2026 match days at Levi's Stadium through Allied Universal Event Services. Every photo on this site was taken on actual match days. Every tip comes from being on the ground, not from reading the official press kit.

Alexandra also edits the Mission Bay News community newsletter in San Leandro, California, and runs Civic Watch San Leandro, a civic journalism outlet covering local government accountability.

This site is independent and not affiliated with FIFA, Levi's Stadium, or Allied Universal. It exists to help ordinary fans navigate match day without the confusion that comes with 68,000 people descending on Santa Clara at once.

Why this exists: The parking walk is real. The transit signs are confusing if you have not seen them before. The sun on the east side is brutal at a noon kick-off. This site tells you what the official guides gloss over.
